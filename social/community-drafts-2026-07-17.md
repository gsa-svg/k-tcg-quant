## A. Reddit r/OnePieceTCG — "PSA 10이 생카드보다 싼 역전 현상" (반응 잘 나올 훅)

**제목안**: The PSA 10 of the OP-13 manga Luffy is now selling BELOW the raw NM price — here's the data

**본문 초안**:
Noticed something weird tracking prices this month. The OP13-118 manga Luffy:

- Raw NM (Japanese retail): ~$15,300
- PSA 10 recent eBay sold median: ~$13,700 (3 verified sales)

Yes — the slab is cheaper than the raw card. Why? The gem rate on this card is 88%, and 3,810 PSA 10s already exist. When almost every clean copy grades a 10, the slab premium doesn't just shrink — it can go negative, because slabbed supply floods in while raw copies at Japanese retail stay scarce.

Worth checking gem rate + population before you pay grading fees on modern OP cards. Happy to share the population numbers for other chase cards if anyone's curious.

## B. Reddit r/OnePieceTCG — "OP-17 예약할까?" (발매 시즌 타이밍, 8월 초 추천)

**제목안**: Before you pre-order OP-17: what actually happened to the last 3 sets' box prices at launch

**본문 초안**:
Tracked Japanese sealed box prices daily through the last three launches. The pattern:

- OP-16: pre-orders peaked ~$220 in late April → $139 release week → bottomed $123 two weeks after release → $150 now. That's -44% from pre-order peak to the post-launch low.
- OP-15: ~$112 around launch → drifted to $88 → back to $98.
- OP-14 (launched cheap, no hype): $63 → never dipped, just climbed to $88 over six months.

The cheapest window has consistently been 2–6 weeks AFTER release, once launch supply floods in. OP-17 is the 4th anniversary set so pre-order pricing will be aggressive. Patience has paid three times in a row.

One wrinkle: OP-17 is the first near-simultaneous global release (6-day JP/EN gap vs ~3 months for OP-13). Part of the old JP premium came from that time gap, so this launch is a genuine natural experiment.
