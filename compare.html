How to read this comparison

The table above ranks every Japanese One Piece Card Game booster box we track. Box price is the current market value of a sealed box, refreshed from live sold and listing data. Card support is the combined market value of the set's top 10 chase cards divided by the box price — a set whose singles are worth several times its box tends to keep getting opened, which removes sealed supply. Demand and scarcity summarize recent sales pace and how few boxes are currently listed. The score blends these signals into a single research number; it is a screening tool, not a buy list.

Where the numbers come from

Box prices combine eBay sold results, active listings with China-region sellers excluded, and weekly ungraded market values. Japanese chase-card prices come from Yuyu-tei retail listings, matched to the exact variant by hand. PSA population and gem rates come from PSA's public population report, snapshotted weekly. When a figure cannot be verified — too few sales, an ambiguous card variant, a missing market — we leave it blank rather than estimate. The full methodology is documented in how our market signals are calculated, and the two most recent data reports are Japanese vs English box prices: six months of data and PSA grading vs sealed supply.
