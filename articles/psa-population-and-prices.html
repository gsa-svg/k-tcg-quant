Research Note

How PSA population data affects One Piece card prices

PSA population reports show how many copies of a card have been graded and at what grade. For One Piece chase cards, two numbers matter most: total population (how many copies exist in PSA holders) and gem rate (the share that graded PSA 10).

What population and gem rate mean

  • Total population — the count of all graded copies across every grade, a rough proxy for how many collectors bothered to submit the card.
  • Gem rate — PSA 10 count divided by total population, showing how hard the card is to pull in perfect condition.
  • A card with low total population and a low gem rate is usually the hardest, and most expensive, PSA 10 to find.

Why low population usually means higher prices

  • Fewer graded copies means fewer sellers competing on active listings.
  • A low gem rate signals real print-quality difficulty, not just low submission volume.
  • Chase cards that combine strong character demand with low population tend to set the floor for a set's overall value.

What can change population-driven prices

  • Population grows over time as more collectors submit copies, which can soften scarcity-driven prices.
  • Print-run differences between early and later reprints can shift gem rate for the same card number.
  • A spike in submissions after a card gains attention can temporarily distort short-term population trends.

How to use this on OP Box Index

Every set guide lists each chase card's PSA 10 sold data and the set's overall gem rate, alongside the lowest verified active eBay listing. Compare population signals across sets on the set guides page or check live numbers on the price tracker before treating any card as a safe hold.
