Basics

How many packs are in a One Piece booster box?

A standard One Piece Card Game booster box contains 24 booster packs. That is true for the Japanese and English versions of every main set (OP-01 through the current release). What differs between the two languages is what is inside each pack — and what you pay for it.

Japanese vs English boxes: the official numbers

  • Japanese booster pack: 6 cards per pack (Bandai official). A 24-pack box holds 144 cards. For the newest set, OP-17 The World's Strongest Warriors, Bandai lists an MSRP of ¥240 per pack (tax included) — about ¥5,760 per box, roughly $37 at mid-2026 exchange rates. Earlier sets launched at lower pack prices, so check the official product page for a specific set's original MSRP.
  • English booster pack: Bandai's US site lists current English boosters at USD $4.99 MSRP per pack — about $119.76 per 24-pack box. English packs contain more cards per pack than Japanese packs, which is part of why the per-pack price is higher.

Two boxes, same set, very different sticker prices: a Japanese box at retail costs roughly a third of an English box at retail. This gap at MSRP is the starting point for the much larger market-price gaps we track — see our six-month Japanese vs English price study, where aged English boxes trade at up to 7.4x their Japanese versions.

What rarities come in a box?

Bandai publishes each set's card list but does not publish official pull rates. Using OP-16 The Time of Battle as an example, the official set composition is 126+1 card types: Leaders, Commons, Uncommons, Rares, Super Rares, Secret Rares, Special cards, a Treasure Rare and a DON!! card. How many of the top rarities appear in any given box is randomized, and premium variants (manga art, special-art parallels) appear at low, unpublished odds. Community case-opening data suggests the rough frequency of those chase pulls, but treat any precise "1 in X packs" figure you see online as an estimate, not an official rate.

This is exactly why chase-card prices drive box prices: if a set's manga rare sells for thousands of dollars and appears rarely, every sealed box carries a lottery ticket for it. Box math: MSRP vs market price

MSRP only matters while a set is in print. Once supply dries up, sealed boxes trade at market price — which for Japanese sets we track moved between $73 and $313 per box as of mid-July 2026, against that ~$37-40 retail baseline. A Japanese OP-01 Romance Dawn box that sold at retail for around ¥5,000 in 2022 traded near $313 (about ¥48,000) in July 2026. The same dynamic works in reverse for brand-new sets: boxes of the newest set often dip below hyped pre-order prices right after release, when supply is at its peak — OP-16's English box fell 53% from its April pre-release price to $188 by mid-July.

Practical takeaways:

  • Buying to open: compare the box price to MSRP first. Paying far above retail for an in-print set means the market expects chase cards to justify it — verify that on the set's top-10 card list before you pay the premium.
  • Buying to hold: the number of packs never changes; what changes is scarcity. Our comparison table shows every box's current price, card support and scarcity signals side by side.
  • Avoiding fakes and resealed boxes: a box that is cheap "because it has extra packs" or has loose shrink-wrap is a red flag — boxes are always 24 packs, factory sealed. Run through our reseal checklist before buying aged sealed product.

Special box types: EB and PRB

Besides main sets, Bandai releases Extra Boosters (EB) — such as EB-01 Memorial Collection and EB-03 Heroine's Edition — and Premium Boosters (PRB), reprint-focused products sold mainly through Japanese channels. Their per-pack contents and pricing differ from main sets and from each other, so check the official product page for each. On the market side they behave like main sets: we track their sealed box prices the same way (PRB-01 was the single strongest Japanese box of early 2026, +113% in six months).

FAQ

How many cards are in a full Japanese booster box?

24 packs × 6 cards = 144 cards per Japanese box.

Is a case the same as a box?

No. A case is a carton of multiple sealed boxes (commonly 12 for Japanese main sets) shipped to retailers. Case prices roughly track box price × count, minus a small bulk discount.

Do all boxes guarantee a Secret Rare or manga rare?

No. Bandai does not guarantee specific rarities per box, and the premium manga-art cards appear at low, unpublished rates. That randomness is precisely what the sealed market prices in.
