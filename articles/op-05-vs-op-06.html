OP-05 vs OP-06: which Japanese booster box is the stronger sealed pick?

OP-05 Awakening of the New Era (released 2023-12-08) and OP-06 Wings of the Captain (released 2024-03-15) are two of the most requested comparisons on OP Box Index. Neither box is a fixed "better" choice — the right pick depends on current price versus recent sold data, which chase cards you value, and how much reseal risk you are willing to accept.

OP-05 Awakening of the New Era

  • Earlier release gives it a longer supply-tightening window than OP-06.
  • Chase-card strength is concentrated around multiple Luffy alternate-art and special-art pulls plus a 1st Anniversary print.
  • Being an earlier set, verified sold history tends to run deeper, which makes price signals more reliable.

OP-06 Wings of the Captain

  • Newer set with a shorter post-release history, so price trends can still be more volatile.
  • Its headline chase card is a Treasure Rare Nami alongside strong Zoro alternate-art and SEC pulls.
  • Slightly larger population base by this stage of tracking means single-listing spikes matter less than for smaller sets.

What the data says that impressions don't

OP-05 is roughly 1.8× the price of OP-06 on the Japanese side ($211 vs $115 sold) — and the gap widens sharply on the English side, where OP-05 sells at $1,005 against OP-06's $269, a 3.7× difference. That English spread is the most important number in this comparison, and it is a supply story: English OP-05 was printed in far smaller volume relative to demand and has been out of print long enough that the sealed market treats it as a collectible rather than a product to open.

There is a signal in the listing column that cuts against the headline. OP-05's cheapest verified Japanese listing is $151.89 — about 28% below its $211 sold median, while OP-06's cheapest listing at $192.38 sits 67% above its $115 sold median. In plain terms: today you can buy a Japanese OP-05 box meaningfully under what boxes actually trade for, whereas Japanese OP-06 sellers are asking far more than the market has been paying. On current numbers the Japanese OP-05 box is the better-priced entry and Japanese OP-06 is a wait.

Population data supports treating OP-05 as the more heavily opened set: 68,854 PSA-graded cards versus 36,818, nearly double. More opening means more sealed boxes destroyed, which normally supports the boxes that survive. Note that OP-05's gem rate is lower (88.9% vs 90.6%), so its graded supply grows slightly more slowly per box opened. Neither difference is dramatic; both sit in the normal band across our full population table.

The honest caveat: OP-06's Japanese sold median rests on only 12 completed sales versus 25 for OP-05. That is a thin sample and one unusual transaction moves it. Treat the Japanese OP-06 figure as directional and re-check before acting; the English figures (102 and 119 sales) are far more solid.

How to compare them properly

  • Always check the sold price trend, not just the lowest active listing — as the table above shows, the two can disagree by 28% in one direction and 67% in the other on the same day.
  • Compare each set's PSA 10 gem rate and chase-card sold data before assuming one set is "safer" than the other.
  • Read what actually moves sealed box prices before treating either box as an investment.
