Is an OP-10 booster box worth buying? (Jul 2026)

The box trades at $80, 10% under its tracked peak of $89 and up 54% over our tracking window — neither crashed nor running. Reprints & original price

OP-10 launched at a Japanese MSRP of ¥5,280 per 24-pack box (about $32). At today's market price that is roughly 2.5x its original retail. On reprints: Bandai does not publish per-set reprint announcements for the One Piece Card Game, so there is no official count. We found no dated reprint record for OP-10 in our sources — which means none surfaced, not that it was never reprinted. See the full board on the market index page.

How fast is OP-10 being opened right now?

Between 2026-06-03 and 2026-07-15, collectors added 1,597 new OP-10 grades — peaking at 258 cards in the week of 2026-06-17, with the latest week at 229 (still running near its peak). All-time, the set counts 13,553 graded cards. Sustained grading volume while the box price holds is the pattern sealed collectors look for: supply burning while demand stays. OP-10 PSA 10 grading data

Across the full OP-10 Royal Blood set, 88.5% of PSA-graded cards received PSA 10, across 13,553 total grades. A higher gem rate means more PSA 10 supply, which usually compresses the premium a graded card holds over a raw NM copy; a lower rate keeps gem examples scarce and the premium wide. That is why chase-card value tracks PSA population and gem rate, not just character popularity.
