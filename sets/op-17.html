OP-17 "The World's Strongest Warriors": release date, box price, and what launch data actually says

OP-17 releases Aug 22 (JP) / Aug 28 (EN), 2026

OP-17 is the One Piece Card Game's fourth-anniversary booster, themed around the Four Emperors and the new island of Elbaf. It is also a structural first: the closest thing to a global simultaneous release the game has had — a 6-day gap between the Japanese and English launches, where OP-13 waited roughly three months and OP-16 two weeks. This page collects the official facts and, more importantly, what our tracked Japanese box price data from the last three launches says about when to buy.

The pattern: hyped sets punish pre-orders. OP-16 lost 44% from its pre-order peak ($220) to its post-release low ($123) in about seven weeks, then recovered to $150. OP-15 drifted down ~21% after launch before recovering. OP-14, which launched cheap and un-hyped, never dipped — it just climbed 40% over six months. The historically cheapest window has been 2–6 weeks after release, when launch supply floods in and hype cools. For an anniversary set like OP-17, expect aggressive pre-order pricing — the data says patience has paid.

The one honest caveat: OP-17's near-simultaneous global release is new territory. Part of the old JP-box premium came from Japanese boxes carrying cards months before the English print existed; a 6-day gap weakens that mechanic. We will be tracking OP-17's Japanese and English box prices side by side from day one — that comparison lives on our compare page and in the JP vs EN data report.

FAQ

When does OP-17 release?

August 22, 2026 in Japan; August 28, 2026 in English, with pre-release events from August 21 (Bandai official).

How much is an OP-17 booster box?

Japanese MSRP is ¥5,760 per 24-pack box (≈ $35); English packs are $4.99 MSRP. Market prices will differ — pre-order asking prices are historically the most inflated of a set's life.

Is pre-ordering worth it?

At MSRP or close to it, fine. At hyped market prices, the last three launches say no: the cheapest window has been 2–6 weeks after release. See the table above.
